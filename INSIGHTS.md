## 📈 By the numbers (as stated on the call)
- **~€80M** revenue · **~2M customers** in 2–3 years
- **180 products** in his original dropshipping "general store"
- The winning slipper sold **12 months straight** vs. a ~**2-month** average product lifespan — the durability signal that told him to brand it
- **~20 influencers drive ~80%** of revenue — found by testing **~200** (a ~**10% hit rate**)
- First accidental creator: **~$10k** cold → **~$60k in a single day** on her 2nd post
- Scaled **$10k/day → ~$100k/day in one month** — then logistics crashed (his cautionary tale)
- **~5 marquee hires in 5 years** — one "gap of the year" at a time

## 🎯 Influencer marketing (his single biggest lever)
- **Stories convert, not reels or posts.** *"Stories is where people want to watch… if you want to convert to purchase, it's stories. Stories is king."* Structure deals around multi-frame story sequences with tight CTAs.
- **The 2-week rights test.** Buy usage rights to a creator's story content for **2 weeks**, run it as a paid ad on your own Meta account, and **scale-or-kill**: works → extend two more weeks; doesn't → drop it.
- **Whitelist with small/mid creators.** Running ads through the creator's own handle (native trust) — small creators agree readily, big celebrities often refuse, so **run both**.
- **Hunt, then hoard, your winners.** When you find a converter, *"don't just move on… I found gold, take care of it. Go meet this person… make these people loyal to you."* Dinners, visits, real relationships = exclusivity competitors can't out-bid.
- **Investigate unexplained revenue.** His first A-player was discovered because revenue doubled with no explanation — so he **literally phoned Shopify customers** asking "where did you buy this?" When you find the source, move at founder-speed (he flew to meet her in person).
- **Vanity metrics are a trap.** *"It's not the way people teach you — engagement rate or follower likes. No, it's completely different."* (See "The Hidden Method" below for a concrete reconstruction.)

## 🛍️ Product & brand
- **"Saturation is king."** A saturated product with **no dominant brand name** is the opportunity. He branded a slipper everyone sold unbranded and *became "the original."*
- **Borrow proven blueprints.** He copied **Davie Fogarty's The Oodie** (basic garment + fun/licensed designs → a ~$550M brand) and ported it to slippers — now signing licensing with **Snoopy, Harry Potter, Coca-Cola, Warner Bros (Batman, Superman)**. Expect **long** approval cycles; big IP holders vet you hard.
- **"Think like a billion-dollar company and act like they do."** Real photoshoots, the same celebrities Coca-Cola hires, high-concept productions — signature moves competitors won't match. (Vision: $1B, citing Crocs at ~$4B.)
- **Sell the feeling, not the feature.** Started with "ergonomic slipper" (pain relief) but scaled by selling **happiness** and a lifestyle. Function converts; emotion compounds LTV.
- **Reposition as you approach a ceiling** — he reframed from "footwear" into "fashion," unlocking new creator pools and higher perceived value.

## 🎬 Content
- **Content must be avatar-deep.** His 3 buyer avatars: (1) **ergonomic / pain-relief** (nurses, people on their feet), (2) **vacation / travel**, (3) **relax-at-home**. Creators must know each avatar's pain, language and lifestyle — *"what they suffer from, how they sleep, how they drink water."*
- **Bring content in-house at scale.** He *acquired a content agency* — an agency juggling 100 clients can't match a single brand's pace once you're scaling.

## 🧠 Mindset
- **Decide fast, adapt in motion.** The general's story: *"The answer is I never know. I just decide… if it was the wrong way, we find out quicker. Don't think, just do — do the thinking during the process."*
- **#1 mistake: deciding from your own taste.** *"The product I thought would not sell was selling… never take decisions on your own assumptions — it's the customer, 100% the customer."*
- **Fail fast.** *"Fail is not even a word — it's a lesson."* You get out-competed if you're slow.
- **Humility as a system.** After ego + sudden wealth nearly cost him everything, he rebuilt around humility and serving the customer over extracting from them.
- **Annual solo planning ritual** — 3 days alone in a hotel each December, pen & paper, business + personal goals.

## 👥 Hiring & scaling
- **Solve one "gap of the year."** Each year a single constraint blocks the next level — fill it with one specialist (his sequence: content → branding → product development).
- **Hire by personal goals, not job specs** — align the role to the person's life ambitions to win loyalty money can't buy.
- **Don't scale faster than your operations.** 10×-ing spend in a month crashed logistics — gate ad budgets behind ops readiness. *"Serve, don't just sell."*

## 🔑 The Hidden Method — how he likely evaluates creators (a concrete reconstruction)
He says his vetting is "completely different" from engagement/followers but never reveals it. The system that fits every clue is **story-first, down-funnel performance scoring** — treat creators as *creative suppliers for paid*, judged by cost-per-acquisition, not clout:

1. **Pre-qualify on fit + story behavior** — audience–avatar match (by country/age), **story views per follower**, link-sticker tap rate, and replies per 1,000 views (intimacy > likes).
2. **Price per story view (CPSV)** — quote ÷ average story views. Model expected CPA *before* booking. *(e.g. 100k story views, ~1% link taps = 1,000 clicks, ~2% CVR = 20 orders → CPA ≈ quote ÷ 20.)*
3. **The real test = their content in your ad account.** Mandatory 2-week paid usage rights; judge by CPA/ROAS and first-frame hold-rate in the first 3–5 days. Kill fast, renew winners.
4. **Whitelist intensification** for small/mid creators — compare brand-handle vs. whitelisted-handle CPA, keep the cheaper.
5. **Confirm with unique codes + post-purchase survey + manual calls** when attribution is murky.
6. **Portfolio management** — a per-creator rolling P&L (organic CPA, ad CPA, AOV, fatigue) and geo benchmarks, which becomes the *"I know exactly which influencers in France"* advantage.

**The big idea:** stop shopping for reach. Pre-qualify by story-consumption quality + avatar fit, set a price-per-view you can make work, then let down-funnel paid performance decide. That's performance media buying with creators as the creative source.
