The playbook

What he actually did

The tactics, organized by theme. Full detail in INSIGHTS.md.

📣

Influencer marketing — his #1 lever

  • Stories convert, not reels/posts. "Stories is king." Structure deals as multi-frame story sequences with tight CTAs.
  • The 2-week rights test. Buy story-content rights for 2 weeks, run on Meta, scale-or-kill; extend 2 more weeks if it works.
  • Whitelist small/mid creators (native trust); big celebs often refuse — run both.
  • Hunt, then hoard winners. "I found gold — take care of it." Real relationships = exclusivity rivals can't out-bid.
  • Investigate unexplained revenue — he phoned Shopify customers to find his first creator.
🛍️

Product & brand

  • "Saturation is king." Brand a proven, unbranded product and become "the original."
  • Borrow blueprints. Copied Davie Fogarty's The Oodie (designs + licensing) onto slippers — now signing Snoopy, Harry Potter, Coca-Cola, Warner Bros.
  • "Act like a billion-dollar company." Real shoots, A-list creators, signature moves competitors won't match.
  • Sell the feeling, not the feature — from "ergonomic slipper" to selling happiness.
🎬

Content

  • Content must be avatar-deep. 3 buyer avatars — pain-relief, vacation/travel, relax-at-home — and creators must know each one cold.
  • Bring content in-house at scale — he acquired a content agency; outside agencies can't match a brand's pace.
  • One product, three messages — customize the angle per avatar.
🧠

Mindset

  • Decide fast, adapt in motion. "I never know — I just decide… do the thinking during the process."
  • Don't decide from your own taste — "it's the customer, 100% the customer."
  • Fail fast — "fail is not even a word, it's a lesson."
  • Humility as a system; annual 3-day solo planning ritual every December.
👥

Hiring & scaling

  • Solve one "gap of the year" — each year a single constraint blocks the next level; fill it with one specialist (content → branding → product dev).
  • Hire by personal goals, not job specs — align the role to the person's life ambitions to win loyalty money can't buy.
  • Don't scale faster than your operations — 10×-ing spend in a month crashed logistics. "Serve, don't just sell."

🔑 The Hidden Method

He says his creator-vetting is "completely different" from engagement/followers — but never reveals it. The system that fits every clue: story-first, down-funnel performance scoring — treat creators as creative suppliers for paid, judged by cost-per-acquisition, not clout.

1

Pre-qualify on fit + story behavior

Audience–avatar match, story views per follower, link-tap rate, replies per 1,000 views (intimacy > likes).

2

Price per story view (CPSV)

Quote ÷ average story views. Model expected CPA before booking — e.g. 100k views × 1% taps × 2% CVR = 20 orders.

3

Test their content in YOUR ad account

Mandatory 2-week paid usage rights; judge by CPA/ROAS and first-frame hold-rate in days 3–5. Kill fast, renew winners.

4

Whitelist intensification

For small/mid creators, compare brand-handle vs whitelisted-handle CPA — keep the cheaper.

5

Confirm attribution manually

Unique codes + post-purchase survey + phone calls when the dashboard is murky.

6

Manage the portfolio

Per-creator rolling P&L and geo benchmarks — the "I know exactly which influencers in France" advantage.

The big idea: stop shopping for reach. Pre-qualify by story-consumption quality + avatar fit, set a price-per-view you can make work, then let down-funnel paid performance decide. That's performance media buying — with creators as the creative source.
